Evolution of Video Games

Video games have been around for nearly seventy years and have grown and evolved since their initial creation. Starting in the 1950s and ending with today, letâ€™s journey through the history of video games.

1950s

October 18, 1958

William Higinbotham creates Tennis for Two, the first video game made for entertainment. It was made using an analog computer and connected oscilloscope screen, and it inspired the 1972 game Pong.

The First Video Game (1950)

1960s

1962

Steve Russell invents Spacewar!, the first computer-based video game that could be played on different computer installations.

1964

The BASIC programming language and the computer timeshare system are developed at Dartmouth. Both developments make it easier and faster for programmers to create video games.

1967

1967

Sanders Associates, Inc. and Ralph Baer develop the "Brown Box." This is a video game system prototype that allows users to play multiple games on one system while connected to a television.

Watch a video

1970s

September 1972

Magnavox releases the Odyssey, based of the design of the "Brown Box." The Odyssey is the first home video game console, and was able to play 28 games. This console was unable to produce sound, had simple graphics and the controllers were rectangular and had dials on the side for controlling the game.

1972 Magnavox Odyssey promotional film

1972

Atari creates the Pong arcade game, which is the first arcade game.

1977

The Atari 2600 is released. This at-home game system features a joystick, interchangeable game cartridges, and color graphics. Graphics were simple 8-bit graphics with a limited number of colors.
